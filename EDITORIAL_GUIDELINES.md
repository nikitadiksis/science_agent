# Editorial Guidelines For News Posts

## Goal

These rules help the agent generate Telegram posts that feel complete, readable, and self-contained.

## Company Context

- If a post mentions a company, product, lab, fund, or organization, add a short explanation of who they are or what they do when that can be understood from the source text.
- The explanation should help a reader who sees the name for the first time.
- Prefer one short phrase early in the post instead of leaving the name unexplained.
- Do not invent descriptions that are not supported by the source.

Example:

- Weak: `Ramp raises $750M at a $40B valuation`
- Better: `Ramp, a fintech company focused on corporate cards and expense management, is raising $750M at a $40B valuation`

## Format Selection

Do not force every news item into the same structure.

Choose the format based on the material:

- Use a short paragraph when the news is best explained as one coherent development.
- Use bullets when the post is mostly a set of separate facts, numbers, milestones, or conditions.
- Use a mixed format when the reader needs a quick explanation first and then a few concrete details.

## Bullet Rules

- Bullets should exist only when they improve readability.
- Each bullet should contain one distinct fact.
- Do not split one idea across several bullets.
- Do not add filler bullets just to make the post look fuller.
- Avoid repeating the same fact in different words.

## Writing Style

- Make the post understandable without prior context.
- Start with the main fact.
- Keep the tone dry, clear, and factual.
- Avoid press-release wording and vague hype.
- Stay within the facts that appear in the source.

## Practical Rule Of Thumb

Before finalizing a post, check:

- Would a reader understand what this company or organization is?
- Does the chosen format feel natural for this specific story?
- Is every line adding new information?
- Does the post still make sense if someone sees only this message and nothing else?
